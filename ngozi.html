About

Ngozi Kalu, PMHNP-BC, AGNP-C, is a board-certified Psychiatric Mental Health and Adult-Geriatric Nurse Practitioner with over 10 years of nursing experience, serving clients across the lifespan. She provides compassionate, evidence-based mental health care, helping individuals regain balance, clarity, and confidence in their daily lives. Ngozi welcomes both local and virtual patients into her practice, where collaboration is central to achieving the best possible healthcare outcomes. She specializes in the treatment of anxiety, depression, ADHD, trauma-related conditions, sleep disorders, and other mental health concerns.

Ngozi works closely with clients and their families to develop personalized treatment plans that align with each individual’s goals and values. Her approach integrates comprehensive psychiatric evaluations, thoughtful medication management, and supportive counseling to help clients feel better, think clearly, and live with purpose. She believes that true healing occurs when care is collaborative, respectful, and accessible.

Education

Ngozi holds a Post-Graduate Certificate in Psychiatric/Mental Health Nursing from Wilkes University, Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania. She earned her Master of Science in Nursing as an Adult-Geriatric Nurse Practitioner from the University of Texas Health Science Center in Houston, Texas, and her Bachelor of Science in Nursing from the University of Texas at Arlington. She also obtained her Associate Degree in Nursing from Lone Star College, Cypress, Texas. In addition to her clinical training, Ngozi holds a Graduate Certificate in Human Resources Management and a Master of Business Administration (MBA) from Westminster College in Salt Lake City, Utah, which strengthens her leadership, organizational, and patient‑centered care delivery skills.

Personal Life

Outside of her professional life, Ngozi enjoys taking morning walks with her husband and spending quality time with her family. She values balance, wellness, and meaningful connections‑principles that also guide her approach to patient care.
